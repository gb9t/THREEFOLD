Personnel Classifications
Classifications are assigned based on proximity to anomalous phenomena — click to expand ›
A
Class A
Personnel essential to Foundation strategic operations. Not permitted direct access to anomalies under any circumstances. In an emergency, Class A personnel are to be immediately evacuated to a secure off-site location. O5 Council members and the Administrator are always Class A.
B
Class B
Personnel essential to local Foundation operations. May only access anomalies that have passed quarantine and been cleared of memetic or mind-affecting effects. In the event of a breach or hostile action, Class B personnel are to be evacuated to a secure off-site location as soon as possible.
C
Class C
Personnel with direct access to most anomalies not deemed strictly hostile or dangerous. Those who have had contact with potentially memetic properties may be subject to mandatory quarantine and psychiatric evaluation. In a breach, non-combatants are to report to secure lock-down areas or evacuate at security's discretion.
D
Class D
Expendable personnel used to handle extremely hazardous anomalies. Not permitted contact with Class A or B personnel. Typically drawn from prison inmates convicted of violent crimes. Subject to regular psychiatric evaluations and administered amnestics or terminated at month's end. In a catastrophic site event, Class D are to be terminated immediately unless otherwise determined by security.
E
Class E
A provisional classification applied to field agents and containment personnel exposed to potentially dangerous anomalous effects during initial containment of a newly-designated anomaly. Class E personnel are to be quarantined as soon as possible, monitored, and may only return to duty after being fully cleared by psychiatric and medical staff.
