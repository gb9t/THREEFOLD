About
Threefold

Here's the plain-English version of what we are, what we do, and whether this place might be for you.

The Short Version

What Is This?

Threefold is a text-based SCP roleplay server. You write your character's actions and dialogue in Discord channels, collaborating with other players to build scenes and storylines. Think of it like collaborative fiction with a bit of structure holding it together.

We're semi-serious — we love good lore and proper RP, but we're also a bunch of people who want to actually have fun. There's no entry exam. If you can write a sentence and get along with people, you'll fit in.

The Setting

We build on the SCP Foundation universe — the wiki lore, the anomalies, the factions — but with our own layer on top. The name "Threefold" comes from a specific in-server event that cracked things open a bit. Three power structures now operate in uneasy balance.

Every anomaly is a thread. Pull one and something unravels. Whether that's good depends entirely on who's pulling.

Is This For You?

You'll Like It If...

You enjoy collaborative writing and want your choices to actually matter. You're into the SCP aesthetic but also want to hang out with people who get it.

Probably Not If...

You want a game rather than a story, or you're looking to "win" RP. We're not the right fit for everyone — and that's genuinely fine.

Rules &
Conduct

Seven rules. None of them should come as a surprise.

—

Staff decisions are final, but they're appealable. If something feels wrong, there's a proper channel for it — just don't do it publicly while everyone's still heated.

01

Be a decent person

IC conflict is fine and encouraged. OOC harassment, targeting people, bigotry, or continuing arguments in DMs isn't. The line is pretty obvious once you're not being heated about it.

Instant ban — no warnings
02

Don't control other people's characters

Write what your character tries to do, not what happens to someone else. This trips up new players a lot, so the first time it's a quiet word, not a punishment.

First offence: guidance only
03

Keep IC and OOC separate

Your character only knows what they've seen in-scene. Using out-of-character knowledge to get an in-character advantage is metagaming, and it makes things less fun for everyone.

Warning, then strike
04

Write clearly

Full sentences, basic punctuation, enough detail that others can respond. Third-person past tense is standard: "She moved toward the door", not "*moves toward door*".

Guidance first, always
05

Content limits

Dark themes and horror are part of the territory. Explicit sexual content is not allowed, full stop. If you're not sure whether something crosses the line, ask before you post.

NSFW: immediate ban
06

Work with staff, not against them

Comply first, discuss second. There's an appeal process — but arguing publicly while things are live just makes a mess for everyone.

Strike — escalates if repeated
07

Show up, or let us know

If you're holding an active scene or faction role, others are waiting on you. Just drop a message in OOC if you need to step away for a bit.

Character archiving after contact attempts

JOIN
New Members — Five Steps

How to
Join

It's not complicated. The only real prerequisite is that you actually want to be here.

01

Join the Discord

Hit the Discord button and join. Read #welcome and #rules in full before doing anything else. Takes about three minutes.

02

Read the lore docs

The #lore-library has faction overviews, active anomalies, site maps, and the backstory. Worth an hour of your time before writing a character.

03

Submit a character

The template is in #character-submissions. Name, background, faction preference, rough personality. A paragraph or two is plenty.

04

Staff review

Someone will look at your sheet within 48 hours. They might suggest small tweaks. Once approved, you get your faction role and full channel access.

05

Jump in

Find an open scene in your faction's IC channel, or introduce yourself in #arrivals. Stuck? Ask in #ooc-general — someone'll point you the right way.
