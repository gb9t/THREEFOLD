SECTION I Briefing · Site Function

Protected Site-07 is the centralized command center for the Records, Archival, and Information Security Administration (RAISA), which handles almost every aspect of the Foundation's technical and infosec concerns. The highly classified facility consists of nine offshore oil platforms that have been extensively retrofitted to support nearly four hundred RAISA personnel and their workstations.

Site-07 hosts the AIAD AI Farm, the PANOPTICON global surveillance network, and the primary servers for the SCiPnet database. Personnel describe it, with no small amount of pride, as "the site at the end of the world."

Due to its Protected status, no non-Thaumiel anomalies are permitted aboard, and no SCPs are contained here. Cybersecurity research, anomalous programming, and machine-learning operations dominate daily activity.

Site-07 maintains a satellite installation on the Alaskan mainland, designated Site-7B and known internally as the RAISA Vault. This facility is constructed inside SCP-6781 (the Deep Well Cave system), hermetically and ontologically sealed using prototype DEEPWELL technologies.

The Vault holds microfilm and digital tape backups alongside the original paper records of millions of Foundation documents that have since been digitized into SCiPnet. The discovery of pre-existing anomalous architecture inside the cave during 1985 construction (Incident SYRACUSE) has been classified at Vault management discretion.

SECTION I Briefing · Site Function

Site-19 is the principal containment and administrative installation for the Foundation's Eastern Seaboard sector, and the largest single facility in active service. The complex was established in 1948 on the Gunpowder Neck Peninsula, occupying the eastern half of the Edgewood Area of Aberdeen Proving Ground, and incorporates more than ten kilometers of legacy Army magazine bunkers, igloos, and underground storage tunnels into its containment matrix.

The site operates beneath the cover of the U.S. Army Edgewood Chemical Biological Center, Foreign Materiel Exploitation Annex, a real DoD billet that has fronted classified work on Gunpowder Neck since the First World War. Civilians passing the gates of Aberdeen and Edgewood, Maryland see only another segment of the Army's largest weapons R&D installation; restricted airspace R-4001 already covers the peninsula, and decades of declassified Edgewood Arsenal experimentation provide a near-perfect deflection for any anomalous activity that does leak. Beneath the cover, six engineered sub-levels descend into Cretaceous bedrock, the upper two interfacing directly with the existing Army igloo complex to allow plausible deniability for surface deliveries.

Site-19 holds hundreds of Safe and Euclid-class anomalies alongside a smaller roster of high-priority Keter objects, distributed across the main complex and a constellation of dedicated annexes. The site is widely regarded as the largest and most important active Foundation installation in the world, and the sheer concentration of objects under one roof has driven on-site security protocols past anything seen at peer facilities.

Site-19 also serves as a primary muster station for rotating Mobile Task Forces operating across the Eastern Seaboard. The peninsular geography, the surrounding tidal marshes, the existing Army outer cordon, and a single causeway approach controlled by APG MPs make it the closest the Foundation comes to a fortress hidden in plain sight.

Personnel describe Site-19 as a small city built sideways into a peninsula. The sub-levels each have their own informal personality (the cafeterias on Sub-2 are infamous; Sub-4 is where you go to be left alone), and most staff measure their tenure by which sub-level they're cleared for that month.

Surface life is rotated through a half-dozen renovated 1940s officer quarters and BOQ blocks behind two layered checkpoints. Summers on Gunpowder Neck are humid, mosquito-thick, and punctuated by hurricane evacuation drills; in winter the Chesapeake hauls dense sea fog over the magazine rows for days at a time. Senior staff with surface clearance can walk the old shoreline batteries on the eastern bluff, where the Bay opens up far enough that on a clear night you can see the lights of the bridge and pretend, briefly, that you work somewhere ordinary.

Despite (or perhaps because of) the heightened security posture, Site-19 staff are notorious throughout the Foundation for their famously casual approach to low-risk anomalies. SCP-294 has its own dedicated Sub-2 break-room corner; SCP-261 is regularly raided for snacks; SCP-131 routinely accompany junior researchers on rounds; and the SCP-529 incident report is required reading for new transfers. Personnel learn quickly that a Site-19 lab coat means knowing exactly which curiosities can be petted and which absolutely cannot.
